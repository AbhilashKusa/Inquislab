We're building AI software for the industries that build everything else.

Refineries, chemical plants, distribution centers, factories — the physical systems the world depends on. Their software is decades old. We're changing that.

How we're hiring

Two teams. Four people. Working in pairs.

We're building one product, Reliability Twin, and taking it to market. That needs two teams: a product team that builds it, and a marketing team that sells it. Each team is two people working together — research + execution, ground truth + creative output.

Team 01

Product · Engineering

Two engineers building Reliability Twin together. One leans toward research and architecture — mapping the problem space, running POCs, deciding how the system should work. The other leans toward execution — writing the production code, shipping the features, owning deployment. Both do both. They pair, they argue, they ship.

Role 01
Engineer · Research
Remote · India
Full-time
Work with founder

You'll be the half of the product team that maps the territory before we build. You'll read the industrial standards, talk to plant engineers, run small POCs to test whether an idea can actually work, and hand clear specs to your pair. You're building the thinking that the code stands on.

What you'll work on

  • Read ISA-18.2 end-to-end and turn it into a concrete product spec
  • Run POCs fast — three approaches in a week, not one perfected in a month
  • Own the data model: alarm events, asset hierarchies, rationalization rules
  • Interview reliability managers to understand the real workflow, not the theoretical one
  • Write the architecture docs your pair builds against
  • Decide the technical direction with the founder — you're not executing someone else's plan

What we look for

  • You've built something that required you to figure out the approach yourself
  • Comfortable with Python, familiar with LangChain and LLM APIs
  • You read research papers, standards docs, or long-form technical writing for fun
  • You can argue for an idea, then change your mind when the evidence changes
  • Bonus: any exposure to industrial data, time-series, or signal processing

Leadership & growth

  • You'll own the research direction of the product from day one
  • As we grow, you lead the research side of engineering — hire, mentor, shape the team
  • You'll learn industrial systems from the inside, not from blog posts

  • The instinct to ship, learn, iterate — not to over-engineer and delay

Leadership & growth
